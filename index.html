Property Investment

Comparing the investment returns of houses and units in Metropolitan Melbourne

Property Types

Houses

For many, owning a house is considered to be the Australian Dream. For this reason, the prices of houses and the land parcels they sit on have increased considerably over time.

Units

Units consist of flats and apartments, which are dwellings without land. They are usually smaller and cheaper than houses, and are often in urban centers such as in the CBD.

Property Prices

Prices

Property prices are largest closer to the city, but have also recently been increasing in popular vacation spots along the Mornington Peninsula.

Prices Growth over Time

Since 2009, House prices have risen much faster than Unit prices because of the increasing value of land. These increses have raised concerns about housing affordability for young people.

Rental Yield

Rents

Renting is cheaper than paying off a mortgage, but at the cost of not working towards property ownership. The proportion of younger people renting has been increasing over time, as many have been priced them out of the housing market.

Rental Yield

Rental yield is the percentage of a property's price that is paid by a renter each year. The income from rent is a key incentive for property investment. However, rental yield has been decreasing over time for houses .

Rental Yield by Price

Areas with higher property prices have lower rental yields. This is likely because of income inequality; The incomes of people who can afford property in higher priced areas are increasing faster than those who can not.

Are Houses or Units Better?

Total revenue

Suppose the price of an investment property grew by 5% in a year, and the rental yield was 3%. Then the overall value of the property would have grown by 8%. This 8% is the total revenue generated by the property.

Is price growth or rental yield more important?

It turns out that there is a tradeoff; with units having higher rental yields and houses having greater price growth. Price growth also makes up a greater proportion of investment value than rental yield.

Are houses or units the better investment?

In general, houses have been better investments than units because the greater income from price growth has outweighed the lesser income from rent. With the trend of decreasing rental yields over time, this effect may be compounded. However, the incomes shown do not take into account the various taxes and costs associated with managing a property
