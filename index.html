Property Investment

Comparing the investment returns of houses and units in Metropolitan Melbourne based on capital growth and rental income

Property Types

Houses

For many, owning a house is considered to be the Australian Dream. House prices have increased considerably over time.

Units

Units include flats and apartments. They are usually smaller and cheaper than houses in the same location.

Property Prices

Prices

Property prices are highest closer to the city but are also high along the Mornington Peninsula. Unit prices are much lower than house prices closer to the city.

Price Growth over Time

Increasing property prices have raised concerns about housing affordability. While the average growth of house prices has been high, it has also been inconsistent and decreased in some years.

Rental Yield

Rents

Renting is cheaper than paying off a mortgage. The proportion of younger people renting has been increasing over time, as many have been priced them out of the housing market.

Rental Yield

Rental yield is the percentage of a property's price that is paid by a renter each year. If a renter paid $20,000 annually to live in a property worth $500,000, then the rental yield would be 4%.

How is Rental Yield Related to Price?

Areas with higher property prices have lower rental yields. This pattern holds for both houses and units. However, properties with higher rental yields also tend to have lower price growth than other.

Are Houses or Units Better Investments?

Total Revenue

Total revenue is the total investment value gained from price growth and rental yield. If the price of an investment property grew by 5% in a year and the rental yield was 3%, then the total revenue would be 8% over the year.

The Trade-Off Between Price Growth and Rental Yield

The slope chart to the right compares the average contribution of price growth and rental yield to total revenue for different types of properties over the last 10 years. It turns out that there is an inverse relationship; with units having higher rental yields and houses having greater price growth. Price growth also makes up a greater proportion of investment value than rental yield.

Are Houses or Units the Better Investment?

In general, houses have had higher total returns than units because the higher income from price growth has outweighed the lower income from rent. With the trend of decreasing rental yields over time, this effect may be compounded. However, the total revenue figures shown do not take into account the various taxes and costs associated with owning a property.
